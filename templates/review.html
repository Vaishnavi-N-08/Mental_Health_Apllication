“Together and in support we have power to heal and live.”- Heidi's Story


I guess my stigma experience is from my family; they know I have anxiety & depression, just not to what extent. It was something not talked about in my family; my parents had the depression diagnosis as well – they never talked about it with us kids. How did you overcome this experience? I discussed it with a couple of my brothers/sister-in-laws back in May, after having a severe panic attack where I drove myself to our local ER. I told them how I felt when it happened so they could get a jist of what I felt like. But told them that it’s hard to explain as the attacks aren’t always the same. Help others by sharing a brief, positive message. Don’t be afraid of being put on meds for anxiety – they can help immensely! Explain to family, friends, coworkers that it’s hard to explain why you feel the way you do but to just be supportive.

“Knowledge IS power.”- AhDee's Story


I’ve been sad since I can remember, the same with being scared around people and often getting sick physically caused by anxiety. No one knew, not even me. I’ve lost job interviews, stopped studying multiple times and was not able to finish. I’ve had anxiety attacks in the middle of a group of people. It COULD be different for other people if we just Make It Ok to TALK about it. How did you overcome this experience? I diagnosed myself with help of the internet and eventually received psychiatric treatment. I talked about it, about how I felt. I gained knowledge. I understood. A decade later I think I AM being properly treated. Help others by sharing a brief, positive message. Knowledge IS power. ((It actually is…))
